Family Weekend on University Community Calendar

Family Weekend has existed for over 70 years. While it is most often associated with a football game, it is also a time to learn more about the campus and the opportunities available to your student! Families of all students are invited to attend–but first year students may be especially eager and ready for family and friends to learn more about their “home away from home.”

If you have a new student on campus this fall, they are probably ready for a visit by this time. They may seem a bit busy and overwhelmed, but they are likely excited to show you about their life on campus.

Family Weekend registration runs through September 5, 2025. There will be no exceptions to this date in order to prepare name tags and materials for your visit.

While you are not required to register, as a registered participant, your name tag will serve as your entry to Family Weekend scheduled events. There will be additional check-in procedures at the Family Weekend Brunch due to the additional cost to attend and limited seating capacity.

There is a $25 per person registration fee. Your student does not need to be registered for Family Weekend with the exception of the Family Weekend Brunch on Sunday, September 28.

The $25 per person Registration Fee includes:

  • Shuttle service on Friday and Saturday ($5)
  • Name tag/lanyard ($2)
  • Family Weekend “Swag” | Including a Clear Bag for Game Day for each registered guest ($10)
  • Light refreshments at the Family Weekend Check-In ($3)
  • Entertainment Opportunities and social events on Friday and Saturday nights ($5)

A Parking Pass will be provided to each registered family at no cost. Parking is limited to the Skytop Lot on South Campus on Friday before 5 p.m., from 7 a.m. until two hours after the football game on Saturday. Registrants may park in open lots on North Campus following the football game on Saturday and all day on Sunday.

Registration opens at Noon on Friday, May 30.

Family Weekend Parking and Getting Around

A Parking Pass will be provided to each registered family at no additional cost.

Parking is limited to the Skytop Lot on South Campus on Friday before 5 p.m. and from 7 a.m. until two hours after the football game on Saturday. Registrants may park in open lots on North Campus following the football game on Saturday and all day on Sunday.

Shuttles will be available from the Skytop Parking Lot on South Campus to bring families to North Campus. There will be two shuttles loops to a variety of residence halls and the Schine Student Center.

Family Weekend Shuttle Schedules

Parking on Fri., Sept. 26 is at the Skytop Lot on South Campus. Family Weekend shuttles will leave from Skytop Lot and drop-off at various residence halls, College Place and the Waverly Avenue side of Schine Student Center between 9 a.m. and 7 p.m. on Fri., Sept. 26.

Shuttles to the western part of North Campus (near the JMA Wireless Dome) will be limited on Sat., Sept. 27 around the football game. Shuttles direct to and from College Place will be available around the football game.

Shuttles will not run on Sun., Sept. 28 as parking is available free of charge in most Syracuse University open lots.

Family Weekend Travel and Lodging

The best place to find information about local lodging is Visit Syracuse.

Residential dining centers and many retail shopping and dining locations will be open and available during Family Weekend. More information about hours will be available this fall.

Please keep in mind that students with an Unlimited Meal Plan have an allotment of guest meals. That is an additional option for your family. Your student must be present with their SUID card for you to use guest meals.

Late September in Syracuse offers the chance for crisp autumn weather with cooler temperatures and the possibility of rain. Temperatures gradually decrease throughout late September, with the average daily high around 67°F (19°C) and the average daily low around 47°F (8°C).
