First-Generation Students

Being the first in your family or support system to go to college can be a challenging task. The Intercultural Collective is here to help you define success, pursue your aspirations and reach your goals.

First-Generation College Celebration Week 2024

Join the campus community Nov. 4-8, 2024, in celebrating first-generation students, faculty and staff through a variety of events! This celebration is to commemorate the signing of the Higher Education Act (“HEA”) of 1965 by President Lyndon B. Johnson. The HEA created federal financial aid programs to aid students’ educations and made key investments in colleges and universities, such as the Federal TRIO programs.

The University’s First-Generation College Celebration Week events are made possible by the collaboration and generous contributions of multiple individuals, offices and units on campus that believe in elevating and celebrating our first generation community of students, faculty and staff.

Syracuse University First-Generation Faculty and Staff

During their time as college students, the following Syracuse University faculty and staff self-identified as first-generation. Composed of faculty and staff members from various backgrounds and disciplines, this list is a visual representation of their dedication to helping current first-generation students build their personal success networks and their eagerness to help grow a campus of support. Students are encouraged to connect with faculty and staff members, who can then share their personal journeys, insight and support throughout this unique student experience.
